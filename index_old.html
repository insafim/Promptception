Abstract

Large Multimodal Models (LMMs) are increasingly evaluated via prompt-based multiple-choice question answering. We show that even minor variations in prompt phrasing can shift accuracy by up to 15%, raising concerns about fairness and reproducibility. Promptception introduces a systematic framework to measure this sensitivity across 61 prompt types and 10 models on 3 benchmarks (MMStar, MMMU-Pro, MVBench).

Our findings reveal that proprietary models are more sensitive to prompt wording, reflecting tighter alignment with instructions. We provide the first large-scale analysis of prompt sensitivity in LMMs and release our evaluation framework to support more robust benchmarking practices.
